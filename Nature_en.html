Flora and Fauna

The Grand Canyon possesses highly developed fauna and flora resulting from centuries of evolution.
Flora
Grand Canyon Park is home to 1,737 species of vascular plants, 167 fungi, 64 mosses, and 195 lichens (According to Wikipedia).
Fauna
355 bird species, 89 mammals, 47 reptiles, 9 amphibians, 17 fish species, and thousands of invertebrate species have been recorded (According to Wikipedia).

Geology

The geology of the Grand Canyon reveals rock layers billions of years old, carved by the Colorado River and testifying to Earth's evolution.
The Grand Canyon exposes one of the most complete and studied sequences of rock layers on Earth. Its ≈ 40 visible sedimentary layers date from about 200 million to nearly 2 billion years ago. At the base are very ancient metamorphic and igneous rocks, forming the basement. Above, the tilted Supergroup and horizontal Paleozoic strata testify to ancient seas, beaches, and fossil deserts. About 5-6 million years ago, the Colorado River carved these layers during the plateau's uplift, progressively sculpting the canyon.
